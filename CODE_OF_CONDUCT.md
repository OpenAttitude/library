# Code of Conduct — OpenAttitude library

This code explains what we value in how contributors and users interact around **this repository** ([OpenAttitude/library](https://github.com/OpenAttitude/library)) and related OpenAttitude panel / FlightGear ecosystem spaces when they concern this project.

It is adapted from [The Document Foundation Code of Conduct](https://www.documentfoundation.org/code-of-conduct/), which builds on the Contributor Covenant and related community policies. We share the same goals: clarity, respect, and safety without penalizing good-faith participation or non-native English.

---

## Our pledge

We pledge to collaborate in a **respectful and constructive** manner and to welcome everyone willing to join us in that pledge. We welcome people regardless of ability, age, background, body size, education, ethnicity, family status, gender identity and expression, geographic location, level of experience, nationality, native language, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, socioeconomic status, or any other dimension of diversity.

Discussion here should focus on **this library**, **OpenAttitude panels**, **FlightGear**, and **free and open source software** in that context. We pledge to make participation a **harassment-free** experience. We pledge to **avoid personal attacks**, **inflammatory language**, and speech that **perpetuates discrimination**.

---

## Our standards

**Positive examples**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language  
- Being kind and civil  
- Respecting differing viewpoints and experience  
- Accepting constructive criticism gracefully  
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project  
- Showing empathy toward other participants  

**Unacceptable examples**

- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances  
- Sealioning, bad-faith arguments, or controversy for controversy’s sake  
- Insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks  
- Repeated baiting or sustained disruption of discussion or events  
- Harassment in public or private  
- Publishing others’ private information without permission (“doxing”)  
- Intimidation, stalking, or violent threats  
- Sexist, racist, homophobic, transphobic, ableist, or exclusionary statements, including “jokes”  
- Excessive swearing directed at people or used to escalate conflict  
- Other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting  

---

## Our responsibilities

**Maintainers** of this repository are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and may edit, remove, or reject contributions (comments, issues, pull requests, reviews, and similar) that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct.

We aim to apply these rules **fairly** and **proportionally**, using the context available.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in:

- This GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, wiki, and related automation)  
- Spaces where you **represent** this project or its community (for example: official project channels, meetups, or social posts explicitly on behalf of OpenAttitude library maintainers)  

It does not replace laws or platform terms of service; it adds community expectations for collaboration here.

---

## Reporting

If you see behavior that conflicts with this Code of Conduct:

1. **GitHub**: If it is safe to do so, you may report via a comment directed at maintainers or via **[GitHub private vulnerability / abuse reporting](https://docs.github.com/en/code-security/getting-started/githubs-security-features)** where appropriate for the type of incident.  
2. **Email maintainers**: You may contact the project maintainer at **<torsten@t3r.de>** for private reports related to this repository. Please use a clear subject line (e.g. “Code of conduct report — OpenAttitude/library”).

Reports will be taken seriously. We will try to respond in a **timely** manner. We will respect **confidentiality** where possible and share details only as needed to understand or address the issue.

Possible responses include warnings, request to modify behavior, temporary limitation of participation, or—in repeated or severe cases—removal from project spaces, in line with GitHub and repository policies.
